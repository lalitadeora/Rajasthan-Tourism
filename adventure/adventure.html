adventures in rajasthan

When it comes to the adventure holiday in Rajasthan, one just can't let go the number of options that can be experienced here. To feel the thrills and chills, paragliding over Jodhpur and Jaisalmer can prove to be completely out of the blue. Feeling of butterflies in the stomach when taking a flight atop some grand forts is simply hard to define in some limited words. There is something about the Rajasthan's beautiful landscapes, and palaces dotted on them; they create a royal aura, and if seen from high above the fray, their beauty doubles up and would rekindle the tourists love for air travel. And that's the kind of worthwhile experience that Hot Air Ballooning offers the visitors. When it comes to hot air ballooning ride in Rajasthan, one should make their way to Jaipur or Pushkar.The landscapes of Rajasthan hosts an equal number of the adventure activities as the sky. It might sound tempting to spend a day camping on Thar desert, sleeping under the stars and doing nothing. But in some corners of these large desert, one can see some ongoing adrenaline-charged activities like cycling and desert safari happening every winter.To further enhance the trip to Rajasthan, try zip lining over shimmering forts. At Jodhpur, Jaipur, and Neemrana Fort, tourists can experience this zip line flight. For some travelers,a nerve wrenching flight might not be the perfect definition of adventure, in fact, it's the stay in the wilderness or roaming around the animals is their perfect idea of a holiday. Fortunately in Rajasthan, tourists can find a good number of forest that are highly acknowledged for offering the world best safari experience. Keoladeo Ghana National Park, Ranthambhore Tiger Reserve, and Sariska Tiger Reserve are the best places in Rajasthan where one could drive through the unique variety of flora and fauna. Rajasthan is best known to the world for its dunes and to encapsulate the best of these dunes; trekking is something that shouldn't be missed. World's oldest mountain range- Aravali, a part of Rajasthan, has been the home ground for trekkers since a long period. Udaipur, Ghanerao, Kumbhalgarh, and Udaipur are some of the best places to go trekking in Rajasthan.
